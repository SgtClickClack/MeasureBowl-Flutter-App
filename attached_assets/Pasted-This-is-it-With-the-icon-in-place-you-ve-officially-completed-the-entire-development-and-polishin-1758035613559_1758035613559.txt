The final phase is to move from development to **deployment**. This is the process of preparing and submitting your app to the world via the official app stores.

Here is a high-level checklist for what's involved, followed by a final prompt to help you generate the necessary marketing text for your store listings.

-----

### The "Go Live" Checklist

Publishing involves preparing your app according to Google's and Apple's specific rules.

#### For the Google Play Store (Android):

1.  **Register:** You'll need a Google Play Console developer account (a small one-time fee).
2.  **Build a Release Version:** You'll run a special command (`flutter build appbundle`) to create a highly optimized version of your app for the store.
3.  **Create Store Listing:** This is your app's homepage. You'll need to provide:
      * Screenshots from the app.
      * Your new app icon (high resolution).
      * A title, short description, and full description.
      * A privacy policy.
4.  **Submit:** You'll upload your app bundle and submit it for review, which is usually quick (a few hours to a couple of days).

#### For the Apple App Store (iOS):

1.  **Register:** You'll need to enroll in the Apple Developer Program (an annual fee).
2.  **Build a Release Version:** You'll use a tool called Xcode to archive and upload the app. The command is `flutter build ipa`.
3.  **Create Store Listing:** Using a portal called App Store Connect, you will provide:
      * Screenshots for different iPhone and iPad sizes.
      * Your app icon.
      * Title, subtitle, description, and keywords for search.
      * A privacy policy.
4.  **Submit:** You'll submit the app for review by Apple's team. This is a more rigorous, manual review that can take several days.
